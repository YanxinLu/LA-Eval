LA-Eval: Evaluating the Long-context Capabilities of Large Language Models in AI-assisted Research Scenarios

First Author*, Second Author*, Third Author*, Forth Author, Tong Zhang
University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign
*Indicates Equal Contribution
Paper Code arXiv

We proposed LA-Eval , a benchmark designed to assess the long-context capabilities of LLMs in multi-turn interactions within AI-assisted research scenarios.

Abstract

In recent years, large language models (LLMs) have advanced significantly in processing long texts, enabling them to handle complex tasks such as summarizing books and extracting information from extensive documents. However, existing benchmarks don't reflect real-world AI assistance scenarios that require multi-turn interactions. To address this gap, we introduce LA-Eval, a benchmark designed to assess the long-context capabilities of LLMs in multi-turn interactions within AI-assisted research scenarios. LA-Eval challenges LLMs to retrieve and synthesize information from a curated collection of 219 cases across 15 domains to generate academic surveys. Our evaluation reveals that even advanced LLMs still have poor performance; Further analysis highlights critical limitations in current LLMs, including issues with instruction following, retrieval accuracy, and hallucinations, offering valuable insights for future enhancements in long-context language modeling.

Overview

LA-Eval aims to evaluate the performance of LLMs in real-world long-text tasks encountered in AI-assisted research scenarios, where retrieving through extensive documents for relevant information and synthesizing it into summaries are crucial. To effectively measure the competencies, we have constructed corresponding stages as follows:


1. Retrieval Stage

In the retrieval stage, the LLM is tasked with identifying domain-relevant papers by extracting paper IDs from a large collection of titles and abstracts. This stage evaluates the ability to perform effective information retrieval and exhibit selective attention in long-context scenarios.


2. Summarization Stage

In the summarization stage, the LLM is tasked with synthesizing information from multiple papers to produce an academic survey. This stage assesses the capability to condense complex and interrelated content into clear summaries, thereby demonstrating its proficiency in long-context understanding and content generation.

Main Results

We use precision, recall, and F1 score to report the performance of the LLM-based agent during the retrieval stage, and use four criteria to achieve a comprehensive evaluation of the generated surveys during the summarization stage, where the dimensions of readability and logicality are used to assess the language-related capabilities of LLMs, while relevance and depth are used to evaluate the domain-related capabilities.

For each model, the input papers for the summarization stage are the papers retrieved during the retrieval stage. Bold represents the best performance among all models, and underline represents the second-best performance among all models.
